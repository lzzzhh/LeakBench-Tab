# Style guide

- Stance: innovation-forward in architecture, conservative in empirical scope.
- Preferred paper name: `CDXR`; benchmark instantiation: `LeakBench-Tab`.
- Preferred title: `[EA&B] CDXR: A Contract-Grounded Evaluation Architecture for Tabular Leakage Repair`.
- Use active, short declarative sentences and consistent C/D/X/R terminology.
- Put the claim in the first sentence of each paragraph and the most important
  qualifier in the final sentence.
- Avoid `novel`, `comprehensive`, `robust`, `significant` without a statistical
  meaning, `solves`, `proves`, `general`, and `real-world validation`.
- Use `shows` for controlled evidence, `suggests` for interpretation, and
  `does not establish` for scope boundaries.
- Report confidence intervals with effect estimates; define every n as tasks,
  keys, seeds, or fixed cases.
- Treat `P3 better` as a bootstrap draw fraction, never a p-value.
- Keep three main tables. Figures must remain colorblind-redundant and use the
  author-selected purple/lavender/coral/gold/sage palette.
